Some specifics

As a new father, I enlisted in the United States Air Force in March 1992. After basic training in Texas, I went to Chanute AFB in Illinois. When the training was over, my first duty station was in upstate New York. Plattsburg was a very cold place, and I had a very hard time adjusting to the large amounts of snow.

After a couple years, Congress decided to close Plattsburg base, and I was sent to Fort Walton Beach, Florida, where I was assigned to a special operations unit at Hurlburt Field. I spent the next eight years there, although I was sent to many different places throughout the world during that time. When I completed my enlistment, I discharged from the military, but stayed in Fort Walton Beach with my family.

The difference in climate from Plattsburg and Fort Walton Beach was incredible. We went from blizzards and sub-zero temperatures to hurricanes and 100+ degrees, and from mountains to beaches. We loved living in Fort Walton Beach.

Eventually, we moved back to Kentucky, where my ex-wife and I are originally from, and had many experiences. I finished college at Western Kentucky University, and earned a degreen in computer information systems, with an emphasis on databases. Unfortuantely, the marriage was not salvageable, so we decided to go our separate ways after our children were grown.
