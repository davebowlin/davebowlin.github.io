David Bowlin: A brief introduction

My name is David Bowlin, and I currently live in south-central Kentucky.

The basics

  • My gender is male
  • Birthdate is 3 July 1970
  • Marital status: very happily divorced
  • I have three daughters and five granddaughters
  • Occupation: software developer

Some specifics

As a new father, I enlisted in the United States Air Force in March 1992. After basic training in Texas, I went to Chanute AFB in Illinois. When the training was over, my first duty station was in upstate New York. Plattsburg's temperatures were cold, and I had a very hard time adjusting to the large amounts of snow.

After a couple years, Congress decided to close Plattsburg base, and I was sent to Fort Walton Beach, Florida, where I was assigned to a special operations unit at Hurlburt Field. I spent the next eight years there, although I was sent to many different places throughout the world during that time. When I completed my enlistment, I discharged from the military, but stayed in Fort Walton Beach with my family.

The difference in climate from Plattsburg and Fort Walton Beach was incredible. We went from blizzards and sub-zero temperatures to hurricanes and 100+ degrees, and from mountains to beaches. We loved living in Fort Walton Beach, but due to some personal issues, we decided it would be best to relocate once again.

Eventually, we moved back to Kentucky, where my ex-wife and I are originally from. I finished college at Western Kentucky University, and earned a degreen in computer information systems, with an emphasis on databases. Unfortuantely, the marriage continued to disintegrate, the kids were grown, and so we decided to go our separate ways.
