Specific Reasons

If I get accepted into the #vetswhocode program for web development, I will learn the languages and techniques to the best of my ability. I intend to be employed in the web development field, and therefore I will be. This opportunity being offered to myself and other veterans should not, must not, be wasted. This is a gift being offered freely by other veterans to help us, and we must do our absolute best to learn and become employable in this field in order to repay this gift, passing on our own knowledge and experience to the next generation of developers.

My children and grown and have families of their own now. Living alone is a new experience for me, but I am enjoying the free time that I have now. I'm getting close to 50 years old and to stay competitive in my career, I must learn new programming languages and excel in them. Completing the #vetswhocode course will help to ensure that I remain self-sufficient in not only my career, but in my personal life as well.

Whether I am accepted into the #vetswhocode program or not, I will continue to explore the many opportunities in web development. I do not wish to remain living in Kentucky for many more years, and therefore I need development skills more relevant to real world technology so that I can stay employable, and that path is clearly web development.

Web development is an incredibly lucrative and fast-growing field. I will be among those who work in it, and am anxious to learn and eventually share my experience and knowledge with other veterans who want a career in software development.
