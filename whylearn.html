Why Learn Web Development?

Although I have been a developer for more than 20 years, my career has been in desktop development. I currently work and live in south-central Kentucky, and have to drive more than 600 miles per week for my job, just getting to and from the office. This wouldn't normally be a problem, but I've recently been diagnosed with MS (multiple sclerosis), and that has made driving extremely difficult at times.

My current job requires me to work on-site, and I cover seven counties in Kentucky by myself. I am the sole IT person in the company, which means I wear a lot of hats: software developer, systems administrator, tech support, etc. The running joke at work is: "If it plugs in or beeps, it must be Dave's responsibility." Unfortunately, this means that I'm on the road even more.

My parents bought me a TRS-80 Color Computer 2, by Tandy / Radio Shack, when I was 14 years old, and I've been hooked on coding ever since. That "trash 80" or "coco" as it is affectionately known, was a turning point in my life. I started coding from the manual that came with it, and I have been writing code ever since. With the rise of the Internet and all the following technology, developers for the Web are in increasingly high demand. As a developer with experience, I am sure that I can learn web programming quickly, and therefore will be able to find an employment opportunity better suited to my physical needs.

Some people go through life doing jobs they despise, or at best don't really enjoy. I'm blessed to have found mine and worked in it for almost all my life. I cannot imagine enjoying a career more than software development. Now, it is time for me as a developer to increase my knowledge and expertise with web development.
